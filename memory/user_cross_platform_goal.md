Karl has decided Mac is his long-term daily-driver — not a stopgap or a compromise, but the desk he sits at every day. The work isn't about making Mac tolerable until something better; it's about steadily smoothing every friction that compounds over years of use.

He came from Linux/COSMIC and still admires that environment's ergonomics, especially for coding. The aim is to **borrow the bits of COSMIC that made coding feel good** — window/space navigation, keyboard-driven flow, terminal/editor/git tooling — and bring them onto Mac. Not to recreate COSMIC visually or recreate the whole desktop model.

**How to apply:** When changes touch the coding workflow (window mgmt, keybindings, editor, terminal, git tooling), bias toward COSMIC-feeling ergonomics. For non-coding macOS surface area (Mission Control, screenshots, launchers, system shortcuts), leave macOS conventions alone unless Karl explicitly says otherwise. Treat small friction-reduction wins as valuable — they compound. Don't propose Linux-flavored rewrites of Mac defaults for the sake of symmetry.
