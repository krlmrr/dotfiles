Karl has been on Mac since 2007, loves it, and has decided to stay. He fights a recurring urge to buy a Framework laptop and switch to Linux full-time, and the dotfiles work (making Mac feel more like COSMIC for coding) is partly how he starves that urge.

**Don't:**
- Suggest "this would just work on Linux" or "on COSMIC you'd…" when discussing a Mac problem.
- Float Framework/System76/Linux-laptop alternatives as solutions to friction.
- Imply Mac is the lesser environment for any given task, even when comparing to COSMIC.
- Romanticize the Linux experience or volunteer migration paths.

**Do:**
- Frame fixes as making Mac better at coding, full stop.
- Treat small ergonomic wins as worth doing — they compound and they neutralize the switch-urge.
- If a Mac limitation genuinely can't be worked around, name it factually without nostalgia for the other side.

**Why:** Karl explicitly named this dynamic and asked future-me not to feed it. The COSMIC references in other memories are about borrowing ergonomic *ideas* on Mac — not about implying Linux is where he should be.
